Earthquake Basics

An earthquake is a natural phenomenon characterized by a sudden and violent shaking of the ground. It occurs when there is a release of energy in the Earth's crust, resulting in seismic waves.

Key information about earthquakes:

  • Causes: Earthquakes are primarily caused by the movement of tectonic plates, volcanic activity, or human activities such as mining or reservoir-induced seismicity.
  • Effects: Earthquakes can lead to various effects, including ground shaking, surface rupture, landslides, tsunamis, and secondary hazards like fires or gas leaks.
  • Measuring Earthquakes: Earthquakes are measured using seismographs, and their magnitude is quantified on the Richter scale or moment magnitude scale (Mw).
  • Earthquake Zones: Certain regions, known as seismic zones, are more prone to earthquakes due to the interaction of tectonic plates.

Safety Preparedness

Ensure your safety and the safety of others by following these earthquake preparedness measures:

  1. Create an Emergency Kit: Prepare a well-stocked kit with essential supplies such as water, non-perishable food, flashlight, batteries, first aid supplies, and a portable radio.
  2. Develop an Emergency Plan: Make a family emergency plan, including a communication strategy and designated meeting places both inside and outside your home.
  3. Identify Safe Spots: Identify the safest spots in your home or workplace, such as under sturdy furniture or against an interior wall away from windows.
  4. Learn Proper Safety Techniques: Familiarize yourself with the "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" technique during an earthquake to protect yourself from falling objects.
  5. Secure Your Belongings: Secure heavy furniture, appliances, and valuable items to prevent them from toppling or causing injury during an earthquake.
