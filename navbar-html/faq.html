FAQ

During an earthquake, it is important to follow these safety measures:
- Drop down to your hands and knees to prevent being knocked over.
- Take cover under a sturdy piece of furniture and hold on.
- Stay indoors until the shaking stops and it is safe to exit the building.
- If you are outside, move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and utility wires.

Earthquakes are primarily caused by the movement of tectonic plates, which are large sections of the Earth's crust that float on the semi-fluid asthenosphere beneath them. When these plates collide, slide past each other, or separate, it can result in seismic activity and earthquakes.

To prepare for an earthquake, you can take the following steps:
- Create an emergency kit with essential supplies such as food, water, flashlight, and first aid supplies.
- Develop an emergency plan for your family, including a communication strategy.
- Secure heavy items and furniture to prevent them from falling during an earthquake.
- Educate yourself and your family on earthquake safety and evacuation procedures.
